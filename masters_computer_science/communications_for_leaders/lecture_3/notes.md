# Simplify Technical Jargon
Benefits of Simplifying Jargon:
- Enables communication with non-technical audiences
- Reduces misunderstandings
- Increases engagement

Strategies for Simplifying Technical Jargon:
- Replace complex terms with plain language
- Use examples or analogies
- Break down process into smaller digestible steps

# Use Active Voice
In active voice , the subject of the sentence performs the action expressed by the verb.
Example: "The manager approved the project."
Active Voice: Subject -> Verb -> Object
Passive Voice: Object -> Verb -> Subject

Benefits of Using Active Voice:
- Clarity: Active voice makes sentences clearer and more direct.
- Directness: It emphasizes the doer of the action.
- Engagement: Active voice is often more engaging and dynamic.
- Reduces Ambiguity: It minimizes confusion about who is responsible for an action.


** Common Scenarios where Active Voicce is Essential
1. Decision-Making Contexts
2. Marketing and Storytelling

** When to use Passive Voice
- When the subject is unknown or irrelevant
