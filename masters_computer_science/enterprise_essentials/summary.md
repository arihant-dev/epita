# Enterprise Essentials - Key Concepts & Definitions for MCQ Preparation

## **FOUNDATIONAL CONCEPTS**

**Economic Agent**
An entity (individual, firm, government) that participates in economic activities by producing, consuming, or exchanging goods and services.

**Economic Value**
A measure of the benefit provided by a good or service to an economic agent; created when needs are satisfied through transformation of resources into products/services.

**Value Creation Process**
Three steps: (1) Satisfying needs of people, (2) Transforming resources into products/services through work, (3) Exchanging/distributing between producers and users.

**Industry**
A group of production activities classified according to the good or service provided; defines companies working in the same domain.

**Market**
An exchange mechanism bringing together buyers and sellers; where prices are established through transactions.

**Supply Chain**
The system and resources required to move a product/service from supplier to customer; includes all transformation steps.

**Value Chain**
Activities performed to design, produce, market, deliver, and support a product; shows how value is added at each stage (Michael Porter concept).

## **PORTER'S FIVE FORCES**

**Industry Rivalry**
Competition among firms providing similar goods/services to the same market; intensity determines competitive dynamics.

**Threat of New Entrants**
Possibility of new companies entering the market to compete with incumbents; depends on barriers to entry.

**Barriers to Entry**
Anything preventing new entrants into a market: regulations, capital requirements, know-how, customer loyalty, licenses, etc.

**Threat of Substitutes**
Products/services from other markets that can replace existing offerings (e.g., streaming vs. DVD, paper cup vs. china cup).

**Bargaining Power of Suppliers**
Suppliers' ability to influence prices and terms; stronger when few suppliers exist or products are specialized.

**Bargaining Power of Buyers**
Buyers' ability to lower prices or demand better quality/service; stronger with buyer concentration or many suppliers.

---

## **COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE**

**Competitive Advantage**
A condition putting a company in favorable/superior business position versus competitors.

**Three Sources (Porter):**
1. **Cost Advantage** - Lower production costs due to scale/efficiency
2. **Differentiation Advantage** - Superior quality or unique features
3. **Specialization (Niche Focus)** - Excellence in specific market segment

**Economies of Scale**
Cost advantages from size/output; cost per unit decreases as production increases (internal or external).

**Economies of Scope**
Cost advantages from diversification; producing multiple products together costs less than separately (Panzar & Willig).

## **BUSINESS GROWTH**

**Five Stages of Small Business Growth (Churchill & Lewis):**
1. **Existence** - Getting customers, survival challenge
2. **Survival** - Cash flow management, breaking even
3. **Success** - Decision point: maintain or grow
4. **Take-off** - Rapid expansion, needs formal organization
5. **Resource Maturity** - Complex structures, formal systems

**Market Growth**
Total demand for product/service increases at industry level; measured by overall sales volume growth.

**Company Growth**
A firm's increasing share of revenue/customers; can occur through market expansion or gaining share in stable markets.

**Internal (Endogenous) Growth**
Organic growth by reinvesting profits, scaling operations, R&D; slower but maintains control and culture.

**External (Exogenous) Growth**
Growth through mergers & acquisitions, alliances, joint ventures; faster but involves integration risks.

**Vertical Integration**
Controlling more stages of the value chain (e.g., manufacturer buys supplier); increases control but capital intensive.

**Horizontal Integration**
Acquiring competitors or adjacent product lines; increases market share and economies of scale.

**Diminishing Returns**
Each additional input yields progressively smaller output gains; typical in traditional manufacturing/processing industries.

**Increasing Returns**
Scale/network effects lead to disproportionately larger gains; typical in knowledge-based/platform industries (W. Brian Arthur).

## **INNOVATION TYPES**

**Innovation (Schumpeter)**
Process of creative destruction where new products/processes replace old ones, driving economic growth.

**Innovation (Drucker)**
Change that creates a new dimension of performance; specific instrument of entrepreneurship.

**Routine (Incremental) Innovation**
Improving existing products/services/processes continuously; "bread and butter" of companies; requires continuous improvement (TQM).

**Architecture Innovation**
Major technology change reconfiguring existing components without altering core design (e.g., mainframe to PC, feature phone to smartphone).

**Breakthrough Innovation**
Finding new markets/business models without major technology leap (e.g., open source, shuttle services, online selling).

**Radical Innovation**
Changing TWO paradigms simultaneously: both business model AND technology (e.g., music streaming, cloud services).

**Innovation Matrix (Four Quadrants):**
- **Existing Tech + Existing BM** = Sustaining/Routine (Incremental)
- **New Tech + Existing BM** = Evolutionary Innovation
- **Existing Tech + New BM** = Disruptive Innovation
- **New Tech + New BM** = Radical/Revolutionary Innovation

**Disruptive Innovation (Christensen)**
Creates new market with different value set, eventually overtakes existing market (e.g., smartphone displacing feature phones).

**Engineer**

**Entrepreneur**

## **ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURES**

**U-Form (Unitary Form)**
Traditional hierarchical structure with functional departments reporting to central management.

**M-Form (Multidivisional Form)**
Multiple semi-autonomous divisions, each with own functions, coordinated by corporate management.

**Hierarchical Organization**
Clear chain of command, vertical division of labor, span of control; Fayol's 5 elements (plan, organize, command, coordinate, control).

**Matrix Organization**
Dual reporting lines: employees report to both functional AND project managers; flexible but complex decision-making.

**Organization by Projects**
Work organized around specific projects with dedicated teams; PMO (Project Management Office) oversees multiple projects.

**Mintzberg's 5 Coordination Types:**
1. **Simple Coordination** - Direct supervision
2. **Mechanistic Bureaucracy** - Standardized procedures
3. **Divisional Structure** - Budget-driven divisions
4. **Professional Bureaucracy** - Qualification-driven specialists
5. **Adhocracy** - Collaboration for unique endeavors

## **CHANGE MANAGEMENT**

**4 P's of Innovation:**
1. **Product** - Changes in goods/services offered
2. **Process** - Changes in creation/delivery methods
3. **Position** - Changes in market context
4. **Paradigm** - Changes in underlying mental models

**Greiner's Growth Crises:**
Each evolution stage creates potential revolution/crisis; management style must adapt to avoid: leadership crisis, autonomy crisis, control crisis, bureaucracy crisis.

**RACI Matrix:**
- **R** - Responsible (does the work)
- **A** - Accountable (ultimately answerable)
- **C** - Consulted (provides input)
- **I** - Informed (kept updated)

**Innovator's Dilemma (Christensen)**
Established companies struggle to adopt disruptive innovations serving niche markets, leading to missed opportunities.

**Competency Trap**
Over-reliance on existing competencies prevents adaptation to changing conditions; organizations fail to learn new approaches.

**Barriers to Innovation:**
Bureaucracy, lack of leadership/vision, poor incentives, lack of collaboration, focusing only on short-term sustaining technologies.

## **MANAGEMENT THEORIES**

**Bounded Rationality (Herbert Simon)**
Human cognitive limitations prevent fully rational decisions; managers make "satisficing" (satisfactory + sufficient) decisions.

**System 1 vs System 2 Thinking (Kahneman):**
- **System 1** - Fast, intuitive, automatic, effortless
- **System 2** - Slow, deliberate, effortful, rule-based

**Five Minds of a Manager (Mintzberg & Gosling):**
1. **Analytical** - Understanding details, decomposing problems
2. **Reflective** - Learning from past, knowing oneself/biases
3. **Collaborative** - Managing relationships, network-style
4. **Action** - Vision, energy, driving change
5. **Worldly** - Cultural awareness, global perspective

**Bureaucracy (Max Weber)**
Organizational structure with formal rules, hierarchical authority, impersonal relationships; most efficient for large enterprises.

**Scientific Management (Frederick Taylor)**
Improving efficiency through scientific analysis of work processes and tasks.

**Human Relations (Elton Mayo)**
Emphasizes social interactions and employee well-being in workplace.

## **MANAGEMENT PRINCIPLES**

**Management vs Leadership (Drucker):**
Management = "doing things right" (execution); Leadership = "doing the right things" (direction/vision).

**Drucker's 3 Management Tasks:**
1. Achieve specific purpose/mission
2. Make work productive and worker achieving
3. Manage social impacts and responsibilities

**Strategy vs Structure:**
"Structure follows strategy" - Strategy determines key activities; structure must align with what business is and should be.

**Fayol's Management Functions:**
Plan (anticipate), Organize, Command, Coordinate, Control.

## **BUSINESS MODEL**

**Business Model Definition**
Describes rationale of how organization creates, delivers, and captures value (economic, social, cultural).

**Business Model Canvas (9 Building Blocks):**

**Top Left:**
- Key Partnerships (external organizations helping achieve objectives)
- Key Activities (essential actions to operate)
- Key Resources (critical assets needed)

**Top Right:**
- Customer Segments (who business serves)
- Channels (how value is delivered)
- Customer Relationships (relationship type established)

**Middle:**
- Value Propositions (unique value offered)

**Bottom Left:**
- Cost Structure (major costs incurred)

**Bottom Right:**
- Revenue Streams (how income is generated)

**Lean Canvas Model (Ash Maurya)**
Refinement focused on early-stage innovation/startups; replaces some Canvas elements to address startup-specific concerns.

## **FINANCIAL CONCEPTS**

**Revenue**
Income from performing activities, usually from sale of goods/services to customers.

**Cost**
Value of money used to produce/deliver something; not available for use anymore.

**Fixed Costs (FC)**
Costs that don't change with output level (e.g., rent, salaries).

**Variable Costs (VC)**
Costs varying directly with output level (e.g., raw materials, hourly labor).

**Average Cost**
Total cost divided by quantity produced; cost per unit.

**Marginal Cost**
Cost of producing one additional unit of output.

**CAPEX (Capital Expenditure)**
Funds to acquire/upgrade physical assets (buildings, equipment); long-term benefit beyond current fiscal year.

**OPEX (Operational Expenditure)**
Ongoing costs for running product/business/system; day-to-day expenses.

**Variable Cost Margin (VCM)**
Formula: VCM = (R - VC) / R, where R = Revenue; measures profitability through variable costs.

**Break-even Point (BEP)**
Formula: BEP = FC / VCM; point where total revenue equals total costs.

**P&L (Profit and Loss)**
Formula: M = R - VC - FC, or M = (R-C)/R, where M = Margin (profit/loss).

**Freemium Model**

## **INDUSTRY EVOLUTION**

**Traditional Industries Phase 1:**
Just-in-time production, Total Quality Management (TQM), Outsourcing non-core activities.

**Traditional Industries Phase 2:**
Personalization, Servitization (services embedded in products), Social/environmental responsibility, Localization, Shorter supply chains.

**Commerce Phase 1:**
E-commerce (online buying/selling), Customer segmentation (targeting specific groups).

**Commerce Phase 2:**
Phygital commerce (physical + digital integration), Customer experience focus, Customer journey mapping across channels.

**Services Phase 1:**
"Poor relation" status, Complementary to products, Often optional.

**Services Phase 2:**
Customer-centric approach, Product-service continuum, Services as core offerings.

**IT Services Phase 1:**
Broadcasting, "Gatekeepers" model (controlled content distribution).

**IT Services Phase 2:**
User-generated content, Peer-to-peer exchanges, Attention economy, Convergence of information/entertainment/advertising.

## **MODERN BUSINESS CONCEPTS**

**Servitization**
Embedding services into products; shift from selling products alone to product-service systems.

**Phygital**
Integration of physical and digital experiences; seamless omnichannel customer journey.

**Product-Service Continuum**
Spectrum from pure products to pure services; many offerings fall in between.

**B2B (Business to Business)**
Commerce where companies sell to other companies; intermediate goods, bulk sales.

**B2C (Business to Consumer)**
Commerce where companies sell directly to end consumers; smaller quantities, greater variety.

## **INTER-FIRM RELATIONSHIPS**

**Outsourcing**
Paying another company to perform part of your work; transferring functions to external providers.

**Innovation Cluster**
Geographic concentration of interconnected companies, research centers, universities fostering innovation; positive externalities from proximity.

**Innovation Ecosystem**
Evolving set of actors, activities, artifacts promoting innovative solutions; network of partners working together.

**Joint Venture (JV)**
Cooperative arrangement between independent companies establishing third entity separate from parents; may involve equity.

**Alliance**
Agreement between firms to cooperate in value-chain activities (R&D to sales); may or may not involve equity.

**Co-opetition**
Cooperating with competitors to achieve common goals while still competing; saves costs, avoids duplication.

**Industry Association**
Organization supporting companies in particular industry, protecting rights, setting standards, representing interests.

- **Opportunities** (External) - Positive factors to seize, market openings

## **DECISION-MAKING CONCEPTS**

**Decision-Making**
Cognitive process resulting in selection of belief/action among alternatives; can be rational or irrational.

**Problem-Solving**
Using generic/ad hoc methods in orderly manner to find solutions; decision-making is problem-solving yielding optimal/satisfactory solution.

**Heuristics**
Mental shortcuts for solving problems from incomplete knowledge; trial-and-error methods finding acceptable solutions in limited time.

**Cognitive Bias**
Systematic deviation from rationality in judgment; personal opinions influencing decisions unfairly.

**Three Mental Models (Opportunity Views):**
1. **Creative View** - Creating new means AND new ends
2. **Critical (Discovery) View** - Correcting errors, new ways to achieve given ends
3. **Logical (Allocative) View** - Putting resources to good use for given ends

## **MARKET STRUCTURES**

**Perfect Competition**
Many buyers/sellers, homogeneous products, no barriers, perfect information; theoretical ideal.

**Monopoly**
Single seller dominating market; can set prices; often subject to regulation.

**Oligopoly**
Few large firms dominating market; interdependent pricing/output decisions.

**Natural Monopoly**
Market where single firm can serve entire demand more efficiently than multiple firms; typical for utilities.

**Monopsony**
Single buyer dominating market (e.g., single employer in remote town).

**Market Concentration**
Degree to which small number of firms account for large proportion of market; measured by N-firm concentration ratio.

## **DISRUPTION & REGULATION**

**Creative Destruction (Schumpeter)**
New innovations replace old, driving economic growth and development; fundamental to capitalism.

**Disruption (General)**
Completely changing traditional industry/market operations using new methods/technology.

**Regulation**
External intervention in markets by governments/authorities to correct market failures: consumer protection, negative externalities, information asymmetry, equity, market power, long-term protection.

**Market Failure**
Situation where free market fails to allocate resources efficiently; justifies government intervention.

## **MISCELLANEOUS KEY TERMS**

**Theory of the Business (Drucker)**
Assumptions about mission (what organization is paid for), about environment, about core competencies; defines organization's DNA.

**Network Effects**
Value of product/service increases with number of users; characteristic of platforms and digital products.

**Attention Economy**
Economic approach viewing human attention as scarce commodity; competition for user attention in digital age.

**Knowledge-Intensive**
Relying primarily on intellectual capabilities rather than physical inputs; characteristic of quaternary sector.

**Globalization**
Increasing interconnectedness and interdependence of world's markets and businesses.

**Market Saturation**
Point where market is no longer generating new demand; 100% penetration reached.

**Industry Standards**
Common specifications/practices adopted across industry; can create barriers or enable compatibility.
